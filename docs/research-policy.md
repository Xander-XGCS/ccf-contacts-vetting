# Research Policy

## Goals

Research should help identify who a person or company is, how they relate to CCF deals or projects, whether there are useful relationship paths, and whether any public-source risk indicators need human review.

## Evidence Standards

Every material claim should link to at least one source. Preferred sources include:

- Company websites
- Public professional profiles
- Government registries
- Court or regulatory sources
- Press releases
- Reputable news
- Public deal pages or announcements

Do not treat search snippets as evidence. Use the actual page when possible.

## Confidence

Use confidence levels consistently:

- `High`: Multiple strong sources or a direct authoritative source.
- `Medium`: One credible source or several indirect sources.
- `Low`: Weak, ambiguous, or partial match.
- `Needs Review`: Identity or claim could materially affect decisions and requires a person to confirm.

## Red Flags

Potential red flags should be descriptive, source-backed, and neutral. Examples:

- Unresolved identity conflict
- Bankruptcy or insolvency result
- Civil litigation result
- Regulatory or licensing issue
- Sanctions or watchlist style result
- Conflicting company affiliation
- Unverifiable claimed role

The system should not make legal conclusions. It should surface source-backed items for review.

## Privacy And Public Repo Boundary

No private contact data, deal documents, personal research files, or credentials belong in GitHub. GitHub stores the system. Google Drive stores the operational data.
